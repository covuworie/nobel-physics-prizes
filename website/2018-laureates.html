2018 Physics Nobel Laureates

Model Predictions

Our logistic regression model had zero chance of correctly predicting the 2018 Physics Nobel Laureates as they were never in the original list of physicists scraped from Wikipedia. Obviously they are now deemed famous enough to have their own Wikipedia entries. So should we declare this part of the study as an epic failure as we were unable to identify the winners? No not quite. Closer inspection reveals many interesting characteristics of the three winners that are related to the top features in our predictive model.

Gérard Mourou

  • Is an experimental physicist
  • Citizen of France
  • 74 years of age (age group 65-79)
  • Has at least 1 physics laureate doctoral student (Donna Strickland)
  • Number of physics and chemistry laureate doctoral students
  • Has 3 alma mater

Arthur Ashkin

  • Is an experimental physicist
  • Worked at Bell Labs
  • Has 2 alma mater

Donna Strickland

  • Is an experimental physicist
  • Has 2 alma mater

Maybe this is a pure coincidence, but more likely, there are patterns in the data that the model has found. Whether or not these characteristics can be attributed to biases in the Nobel Physics Prize nomination and selection process is another matter, as correlation does not necessarily imply causation.
